01 - Economic Base

Current economic development base

Economic development work is split across state incentive programs, county strategy, city approvals, utility capacity, workforce institutions, airport and rail assets, and private-sector recruitment.

Geography

Tucson to Nogales corridor

Tucson is roughly 64 miles from Nogales. I-19, Union Pacific rail, the Mariposa Port of Entry, and the Port of Tucson connect the region to cross-border trade.

Assets

Defense, university, and logistics base

Raytheon, Davis-Monthan, the University of Arizona, the airport, I-10, I-19, and Port of Tucson form a strong platform.

Approvals

Project review path

Large projects may require site control, zoning, infrastructure agreements, utility commitments, incentives, and workforce training support.

Measures

Current measures

Common measures include jobs, wages, capital investment, occupied industrial space, exports, business starts, and labor-force participation.

02 - Target Sectors

Target sectors named for regional strategy

Employer bases, workforce links, infrastructure assets, and state incentive tools.

AD

Aerospace & Defense

Major anchors include defense contractors, Davis-Monthan Air Force Base, airport assets, and federal procurement activity.

AM

Advanced Manufacturing

Includes battery, optics, precision manufacturing, clean technology, and supplier projects.

CM

Critical Minerals

Copper, mining technology, and materials supply chains connect to energy, semiconductor, and electric-vehicle production.

HB

Health & Bioscience

Healthcare employment, UA Health Sciences, medical research, and bioscience facilities anchor this sector.

TL

Trade & Logistics

Border proximity, Port of Tucson, interstates, airport access, and Sonora trade relationships support this sector.

ET

Energy Technology

Includes battery production, grid equipment, clean-energy technology, utility planning, and university research.

03 - Authority Map

How projects move through public systems

Economic development projects move through state program eligibility, local site approvals, infrastructure checks, incentive agreements, and workforce-program coordination.

01

State framework

Arizona Commerce Authority programs include tax-credit, grant, site-selection, and workforce-training tools with defined eligibility rules.

02

County strategy

Pima County works on strategic sites, infrastructure readiness, county approvals, and foreign-direct-investment outreach.

03

City tools

Tucson manages city approvals, development services, public infrastructure agreements, and local incentive tools such as the Primary Jobs Incentive.

04

Private navigation

The Chamber and industry groups connect employers with public agencies, utilities, training providers, and site contacts.

05

Talent pipeline

UA, Pima Community College, JTED, and workforce boards provide training programs and employer partnerships.

04 - Policy Initiatives

Current economic development initiatives

Programs, plans, and tools used in project attraction and retention.

Local Capacity

Tucson economic development consolidation

  • Tucson's Primary Jobs Incentive and local development agreements can support qualifying primary-employer projects.
  • City economic development consolidation affects prospect intake, permit coordination, incentive agreements, and public infrastructure commitments.
Regional Strategy

Pima County economic development plan

  • Pima County economic development plans identify target sectors, strategic sites, infrastructure needs, and business retention work.
  • County policy tools include site control, infrastructure participation, workforce partnerships, and foreign-direct-investment outreach.
Investment Pipeline

Foreign-direct-investment outreach

  • Foreign-direct-investment work includes trade missions, delegation visits, site promotion, supplier relationships, and binational coordination.
  • Mexico, Taiwan, Sweden, and other international markets connect to specific sectors such as trade, logistics, aerospace, mining, optics, and advanced manufacturing.
Site Readiness

County-owned strategic sites

  • Strategic sites include county-owned or logistics-oriented industrial properties tied to I-10, I-19, rail, airport, and border access.
  • Site policy turns on zoning, water, sewer, power, roads, rail access, environmental review, and any public infrastructure agreement.
