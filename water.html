01 - Authority Map

Where water decisions are made

Water authority is split across federal river operations, Arizona groundwater law, state agencies, local utilities, tribal rights, and city or county land-use approvals.

Federal

Colorado River framework

The Bureau of Reclamation, basin states, and the Central Arizona Project affect Colorado River deliveries and shortage rules.

State

ADWR and AMAs

The Arizona Department of Water Resources administers Active Management Areas, groundwater rules, and Assured Water Supply determinations.

Legislature

State statutes and funding

The Legislature changes water statutes and creates funding tools. WIFA finances water infrastructure, conservation, and augmentation projects.

Local

Utilities and land use

Tucson Water, other providers, councils, supervisors, and development departments connect service capacity to land-use approvals.

02 - Policy Initiatives

Current water policy areas

These are the main rulemaking, statute, funding, and legal areas affecting water availability in Southern Arizona.

Growth Capacity

Assured Water Supply Rulemaking

  • Arizona's Assured Water Supply program requires most new subdivisions in Active Management Areas to show a 100-year water supply.
  • ADWR certificates, municipal designations, Alternative Designation of Assured Water Supply rules, and groundwater-model updates affect platting and permits.
Conservation

Ag-to-Urban Groundwater Transition

  • Arizona's ag-to-urban groundwater conservation tools allow some retired agricultural use to support municipal or development demand under ADWR review.
  • Policy details turn on location, water-right type, measured pumping reduction, credit duration, and enforceability.
Macro Risk

Post-2026 Colorado River Framework

  • The 2007 Colorado River Interim Guidelines expire after 2026, and the Bureau of Reclamation is preparing replacement operating rules.
  • Central Arizona Project deliveries, shortage tiers, storage credits, and exchange agreements are connected to the final federal framework.
Legal Exposure

Groundwater and River Cases

  • Groundwater pumping, surface-water impacts, tribal water rights, and protected river systems can trigger legal constraints.
  • Court orders, settlements, and quantified tribal water-right agreements can change pumping, mitigation, or permitting requirements.
